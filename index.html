§ 01
On Cargo

Filed from the wheelhouse, 03:14 ship's time. Transcribed by the second mate.

Every voyage is a small act of faith — a wager against weather, ledger, and time. The Meridian carries twenty-two thousand containers across ocean corridors older than any nation she flies.

We do not move boxes. We move the promises packed inside them: grain for Lagos, turbines for Dakar, medicine for ports whose names our grandfathers never learned to spell.

The route from Rotterdam to Singapore crosses four seas, one canal, and a thousand quiet hours where nothing happens but the low hum of the engine and the turning of pages in the mess. This is the texture of the trade: long stretches of ordinary, punctuated by the exacting choreography of port.

Meridian Line keeps a fleet of seventeen vessels. They sail under a plain white flag with a single rust-coloured stripe — our reminder that the work is unglamorous, dependable, and absolutely necessary.
